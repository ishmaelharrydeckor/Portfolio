Key Achievements

Eco-Friendly Fufu Machine Prototype

2021 4 min read Volta Region, Ghana

Project Overview

Designed and manufactured a prototype of an eco-friendly fufu machine to address environmental concerns and food safety issues associated with traditional fuel-based models. This innovation earned 4th Place in the Volta Region STEM Competition, demonstrating practical engineering solutions to local challenges.

Problem Statement

Traditional fufu machines rely on fossil fuels, leading to:

  • High emissions contributing to environmental pollution
  • Food contamination risks from fuel residues
  • Health concerns for operators and consumers
  • Operational costs and maintenance challenges

Innovation & Design Process

1

Research & Analysis

Conducted thorough research on existing fufu machine designs, identifying inefficiencies and environmental impacts. Analyzed alternative energy sources suitable for the Ghanaian context.

2

Conceptual Design

Developed design concepts focusing on clean energy integration, improved food safety mechanisms, and sustainable operation. Considered cost-effectiveness for local adoption.

3

Prototype Development

Manufactured a functional prototype incorporating eco-friendly technologies and food safety features. Tested and refined the design through multiple iterations.

4

Testing & Validation

Conducted comprehensive testing to verify performance, safety, and environmental benefits. Compared results with traditional models to demonstrate improvements.

Key Features

  • Reduced emissions through clean energy integration
  • Enhanced food safety mechanisms preventing contamination
  • Improved efficiency and performance
  • Cost-effective operation and maintenance
  • User-friendly design for local adoption

Recognition

4th Place, Volta Region STEM Competition

Recognized for innovation, practical application, and potential environmental impact. The project demonstrated engineering excellence and commitment to sustainable solutions for local challenges.

STEM Vending Machine Prototype

2021 4 min read Mawuli School, Ho

Project Overview

Innovated solutions to overcome resource limitations by sourcing alternative components to build a functional vending machine prototype. This project won the inter-class STEM competition, demonstrating exceptional resourcefulness, engineering design skills, and creative problem-solving.

Challenge

The primary challenge was building a functional vending machine with limited resources and budget constraints typical of school STEM competitions. Standard components were either unavailable or too expensive, requiring innovative alternatives.

Innovative Solutions

1

Component Sourcing

Identified alternative, locally-available components that could serve the same functions as standard parts. This required deep understanding of mechanical and electrical systems.

2

Adaptation & Modification

Modified and adapted alternative components to fit the vending machine design. This involved creative engineering solutions and hands-on fabrication skills.

3

System Integration

Integrated all components into a cohesive, functional system. Ensured proper operation of dispensing mechanisms, payment systems, and user interface.

4

Testing & Refinement

Conducted extensive testing to ensure reliability and functionality. Made iterative improvements based on test results.

Key Skills Demonstrated

  • Resourceful problem-solving under constraints
  • Mechanical and electrical engineering design
  • System integration and programming
  • Prototyping and fabrication skills
  • Iterative design and improvement processes

Recognition

Winner, Inter-Class STEM Competition

Awarded 1st place for demonstrating exceptional innovation, resourcefulness, and engineering design skills. The project showcased the ability to overcome limitations through creative problem-solving and technical expertise.

Impact & Learning

This project reinforced the importance of resourcefulness and creative thinking in engineering. It demonstrated that limitations can be catalysts for innovation when approached with the right mindset and technical knowledge. The experience contributed significantly to my engineering education and problem-solving capabilities.
